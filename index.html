About

I'm a Ph.D. candidate in Electrical and Computer Engineering at Drexel University, where I design and co-design high-performance, low-power ASIC accelerators for some of computing's hardest problems.

My research sits at the intersection of physics-inspired computing, chip design, and algorithm optimization. I build oscillator-based Ising machines in silicon and software — architecting the hardware, writing the kernels, and running them end-to-end.

I've shipped implementations across the full stack: custom CUDA kernels on GPUs, JAX-accelerated solvers on TPUs, and full RTL-to-GDSII ASIC tapeouts. I care about making every transistor count.
